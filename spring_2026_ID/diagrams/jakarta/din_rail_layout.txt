    ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
     NOTES
    ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

    1. DIN rails cut to ~340 mm to fit inside 370 mm internal width
       with clearance for cable routing on both sides.

    2. Upper rail mounts ~60 mm from top of backplate.
       Lower rail mounts ~60 mm below upper rail (center-to-center).

    3. SPD (surge protector) goes FIRST on upper rail (leftmost).
       AC mains enters from left side cable gland, hits SPD before
       anything else.

    4. SDR-120-12 PSU is AC-powered. All components to its right
       on the upper rail are DC (12V bus).

    5. PoE switch powers TWO cameras (vs. one at Sukabumi).
       No relay needed for Jakarta - cameras run continuously
       on AC power.

    6. Pi 5 + G469 mounted via CLIP-RPI DIN adapter + C45 clips
       for the G469 board.

    7. Leave cable routing space between rails and at right side
       of each rail (free space area).

    8. Aluminum DIN clips (e.g., Phoenix Contact 1201578) used for
       components without built-in DIN mounting (fuses, PoE switch,
       modem).

    9. End stops prevent lateral sliding during transport/vibration.

   10. Route AC wiring along upper-left only (SPD to PSU).
       Route 12V DC wiring along remainder of upper rail.
       Route USB, Ethernet, and GPIO wiring along lower rail.

   11. Samsung FIT Plus USB drive plugs directly into Pi USB-A port
       (no DIN rail space needed).

   12. See jakarta_overview.svg for electrical connections between
       these components.
