    ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
     NOTES
    ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

    1. DIN rails cut to ~340 mm to fit inside 370 mm internal width
       with clearance for cable routing on both sides.

    2. Upper rail mounts ~60 mm from top of backplate.
       Lower rail mounts ~60 mm below upper rail (center-to-center).

    3. Leave cable routing space between rails and at right side
       of each rail (free space area).

    4. Pi 5 + G469 is the tallest stack (~55 mm). Ensure clearance
       to enclosure lid (203 mm depth).

    5. Aluminum DIN clips (e.g., Phoenix Contact 1201578) used for
       components without built-in DIN mounting (fuses, PoE switch,
       modem).

    6. End stops prevent lateral sliding during transport/vibration.

    7. Route 12V power wiring along upper rail. Route USB, Ethernet,
       and GPIO wiring along lower rail. Cross between rails at
       left side near TB1.

    8. Samsung FIT Plus USB drive plugs directly into Pi USB-A port
       (no DIN rail space needed).

    9. See circuit_diagram.txt for electrical connections between
       these components.
