# 8.3 Camera Installation and Mounting

## Overview

Camera installation is a critical phase that directly impacts image quality, measurement accuracy, and system reliability. Proper mounting ensures the camera remains stable, properly aimed, and protected from weather while capturing clear images of the river and staff gauge. This section provides detailed procedures for mounting structure preparation, camera installation, weatherproofing, and initial configuration.

**Time Required:** 2-4 hours

**Prerequisites:**
- Power system installed and tested (Section 8.1 or 8.2)
- Mounting structure installed during site preparation
- Camera position and aim determined during site survey (Chapter 7)
- All equipment and tools available
- Weather conditions suitable for installation (dry, moderate wind)

## Safety Considerations

**SAFETY WARNINGS:**

- **Working at Height:** Use appropriate fall protection when installing cameras on poles or elevated structures. Never work alone on elevated installations.
- **Electrical Hazards:** Camera may receive power. Verify power is disconnected before making electrical connections.
- **Weather Exposure:** Do not install cameras in rain, high winds, or lightning conditions.
- **Tool Safety:** Use tools appropriate for the task. Secure all tools when working at height.
- **Heavy Equipment:** Camera housings can be heavy. Use proper lifting techniques and get assistance when needed.

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):**
- Hard hat
- Safety glasses
- Work gloves
- Safety harness and lanyard (for work above 2 meters)
- Closed-toe work boots
- Tool belt with secure attachments

**Camera System Components:**
- Camera in weatherproof housing

- Desiccant packets (silica gel for moisture control)
- Sunshade or hood (if not integrated in housing)
- Anti-fog coating or inserts (optional)

**Weatherproofing Materials:**
- Silicone sealant (outdoor rated, clear or neutral)
- Self-amalgamating tape (rubber splicing tape)
- Cable ties (UV resistant)
- Protective conduit or cable tray
- Heat shrink tubing
- Waterproof junction box (if needed for connections)
- Thread sealant tape (PTFE)

**Tools Required:**
- Adjustable wrench or socket set
- Allen keys (hex keys)
- Screwdrivers (Phillips and flat)
- Drill with bits (if additional mounting holes needed)
- Level (bubble or digital)
- Compass or smartphone compass app
- Measuring tape

- Sealant application gun
- Crimping tool (for network cables if terminating)
- Multimeter
- Laptop or tablet (for camera configuration)
- Safety rope or lanyard for tools

## Step 1: Mounting Structure Preparation

### 1.1 Inspect Mounting Structure

**Procedure:**

1. **Visual Inspection:**
   - Check all welds or joints for cracks or damage
   - Verify all bolts are tight
   - Inspect for rust or corrosion
   - Confirm structure is plumb and level
   - Check that structure is secure in ground or building

2. **Load Verification:**
   - Verify structure can support camera weight plus wind loading
   - Typical camera system: 2-5 kg
   - Wind loading depends on camera surface area and local wind speeds
   - Apply moderate force to structure; should not flex significantly

3. **Clearance Check:**
   - Verify no obstructions block camera view
   - Confirm mounting arm extends sufficiently from pole
   - Check clearance for camera adjustment
   - Ensure adequate space for cable routing

**Quality Check:**
- Structure is solid with no movement
- All mounting surfaces are clean and free of debris
- Structure orientation matches site survey plan
- Adequate clearance for camera installation

### 1.2 Clean Mounting Surfaces

**Procedure:**

1. **Remove Debris:**
   - Brush away dirt, dust, and cobwebs
   - Remove any rust with wire brush
   - Clean mounting surfaces with cloth

2. **Prepare Mounting Points:**
   - Ensure mounting holes are clear
   - Check threads are clean (if using threaded holes)
   - Apply anti-seize compound to bolts if mounting to metal

3. **Apply Corrosion Protection:**
   - If mounting surface is bare metal, apply rust inhibitor
   - Allow to dry before mounting camera
   - Consider painting exposed metal surfaces

### 1.3 Verify Mounting Bracket Compatibility

**Procedure:**

1. **Test Fit Mounting Bracket:**
   - Position bracket on mounting structure
   - Verify bolt holes align
   - Check that bracket orientation allows proper camera aiming
   - Ensure bracket provides adequate support

2. **Check Adjustment Range:**
   - Verify bracket allows required pan adjustment
   - Verify bracket allows required tilt adjustment
   - Confirm locking mechanisms function smoothly
   - Test all adjustment axes

3. **Prepare Mounting Hardware:**
   - Select appropriate bolt size and length
   - Use stainless steel hardware to prevent corrosion
   - Prepare lock washers or thread-locking compound
   - Have all tools ready for installation

## Step 2: Camera Bracket Installation

### 2.1 Position Mounting Bracket

**Reference Site Survey Plan:**
- Camera height: _____ meters above ground
- Camera aim azimuth: _____ degrees
- Camera tilt angle: _____ degrees (from horizontal)
- Distance to target (staff gauge): _____ meters

**Procedure:**

1. **Mark Bracket Position:**
   - Measure to correct height on mounting structure
   - Mark mounting hole locations
   - Use level to ensure bracket will be horizontal (or at correct angle)
   - Verify position allows camera to aim at target

2. **Position Bracket:**
   - Lift bracket into position
   - Align mounting holes
   - Insert bolts loosely (do not tighten yet)
   - Verify bracket position and orientation

3. **Level Bracket:**
   - Use level to check bracket is horizontal
   - Adjust as needed
   - Tighten one bolt to hold position
   - Recheck level before tightening remaining bolts

### 2.2 Secure Mounting Bracket

**Procedure:**

1. **Tighten Mounting Bolts:**
   - Apply thread-locking compound or use lock washers
   - Tighten bolts in star pattern (for brackets with multiple bolts)
   - Use torque wrench if torque specification provided
   - Typical torque: 15-25 Nm (check bracket specifications)
   - Verify bracket does not rotate or move under hand pressure

2. **Verify Installation:**
   - Check all bolts are tight
   - Verify bracket is level and secure
   - Test bracket adjustment mechanisms
   - Confirm bracket can support camera weight

**Quality Check:**
- Bracket is securely mounted
- No movement when moderate force applied
- Level and properly oriented
- All adjustment mechanisms function smoothly

## Step 3: Camera Attachment and Aiming

### 3.1 Prepare Camera

**BEFORE going to elevated installation location:**

1. **Inspect Camera:**
   - Check housing for damage
   - Verify lens is clean and protective cover removed
   - Check that all housing seals are intact
   - Verify desiccant is fresh (blue color for silica gel)

2. **Configure Basic Settings (if possible):**
   - Connect camera to laptop via network cable
   - Access camera web interface
   - Set IP address (static recommended)
   - Configure image resolution (match site plan)
   - Set frame rate (typically 1-5 fps for monitoring)
   - Configure compression settings
   - Note: Full configuration done after mounting (Section 8.4)

3. **Prepare Cable Connections:**
   - Identify power connection type (PoE or separate DC power)
   - Prepare network cable (test cable for continuity)
   - If crimping connectors, do this at ground level
   - Label all cables clearly

4. **Install Desiccant:**
   - Place fresh desiccant packets inside camera housing
   - Typical: 1-2 packets for small housing, 3-4 for large housing
   - Note date on housing exterior (replace desiccant every 6-12 months)

### 3.2 Mount Camera to Bracket

**SAFETY:** Use tool lanyard to secure camera when working at height.

**Procedure:**

1. **Attach Camera Mounting Adapter:**
   - Most cameras have standard mounting thread (1/4"-20 or 3/8"-16)
   - Attach mounting plate to camera base
   - Tighten securely (hand tight plus 1/4 turn with wrench)
   - Verify mounting plate is secure

2. **Lift Camera to Bracket:**
   - Use tool lanyard to secure camera before releasing grip
   - Position camera on bracket mounting surface
   - Align mounting holes or attachment points
   - Insert mounting bolts loosely

3. **Initial Orientation:**
   - Point camera generally toward target area
   - Adjust pan angle roughly to aim at staff gauge
   - Adjust tilt angle roughly (typically 5-20 degrees down from horizontal)
   - Do not tighten fully yet (will fine-tune aim next)

### 3.3 Aim Camera at Target

**Reference Site Survey:**
- Target location (staff gauge): _____ degrees azimuth, _____ meters distance
- Desired field of view: _____ meters width at gauge location
- Vertical coverage: _____ meters (include high and low water marks)

**Procedure:**

1. **Coarse Aiming - Azimuth (Horizontal):**
   - Use compass to verify camera points toward target
   - Adjust pan angle on bracket
   - Align camera axis with staff gauge location
   - Account for perspective if gauge is off to one side

2. **Coarse Aiming - Tilt (Vertical):**
   - Estimate vertical angle to center of desired view
   - Adjust tilt angle on bracket
   - Typical tilt: 5-15 degrees below horizontal for elevated cameras
   - Consider: Camera center should aim slightly above gauge center (accounts for wider horizontal FOV than vertical)

3. **Verify Aiming with Test Image:**
   - Connect laptop to camera (via long network cable or wireless if available)
   - Access camera live view
   - Check that staff gauge is visible in frame
   - Verify vertical coverage includes all gauge markings
   - Adjust aim as needed

4. **Fine-Tune Aiming:**
   - Make small adjustments to center gauge in image
   - Ensure gauge is positioned in central portion of image (best lens quality)
   - Verify horizon is level (rotate camera on mounting if needed)
   - Leave some margin around gauge for water level variations

5. **Lock Camera Position:**
   - Tighten all bracket adjustment bolts
   - Use thread-locking compound on adjustment bolts
   - Recheck aim after tightening (may shift slightly)
   - Make final minor adjustments if needed
   - Verify all locking mechanisms are secure

**Quality Check:**
- Staff gauge clearly visible in image
- Gauge positioned in central area of frame
- Entire gauge length visible (high and low water marks)
- Horizon is level (or intentionally tilted if required)
- Camera is locked securely (no movement)

### 3.4 Focus and Image Quality Check

**Procedure:**

1. **Adjust Focus (if manual focus):**
   - Access camera focus adjustment (may be in housing or via software)
   - Zoom to maximum zoom level
   - Focus on staff gauge markings
   - Verify numbers are sharp and readable
   - Return to normal zoom level

2. **Check Image Quality:**
   - Verify image is sharp and clear
   - Check for lens obstructions or dirt
   - Verify adequate brightness (adjust exposure if needed)
   - Check for reflections or glare (may need sunshade adjustment)

3. **Verify Coverage:**
   - Confirm all required gauge markings visible
   - Check field of view covers expected water level range
   - Verify perspective is acceptable (minimal distortion)

**Example Image Quality Criteria:**
- Gauge markings readable at full resolution
- Numbers clearly distinguishable
- No motion blur (for still water surface)
- Adequate contrast between gauge and background

## Step 4: Weatherproofing

### 4.1 Cable Entry Sealing

**Critical:** All cable entry points must be completely waterproof.

**Procedure:**

1. **Install Cable Glands:**
   - Route cables through cable glands in housing
   - Ensure gland size matches cable diameter
   - Tighten gland nut to compress seal around cable
   - Verify cable cannot pull through gland

2. **Create Drip Loops:**
   - Form cable into downward loop before entry to housing
   - Lowest point of loop should be below entry point
   - Secure loop with cable tie to structure
   - This prevents water from following cable into housing

3. **Seal Cable Entry:**
   - Apply silicone sealant around cable gland threads
   - Apply sealant at cable entry point on housing
   - Smooth sealant to create water-shedding slope
   - Allow sealant to cure per manufacturer instructions (typically 24 hours for full cure)

**Quality Check:**
- Cable glands are tight
- Drip loops formed correctly
- No gaps around cable entries
- Sealant applied smoothly

### 4.2 Housing Seal Verification

**Procedure:**

1. **Inspect Housing Seals:**
   - Check rubber gasket is properly seated in groove
   - Verify no dirt or debris on sealing surfaces
   - Ensure gasket is not pinched or twisted
   - Check for any damage to gasket

2. **Clean Sealing Surfaces:**
   - Wipe housing seal surfaces with clean cloth
   - Remove any dirt, oil, or residue
   - Do not use petroleum-based products on rubber seals
   - Allow to dry before closing housing

3. **Close Housing:**
   - Position cover carefully to avoid pinching gasket
   - Align cover with housing body
   - Insert and tighten all mounting screws
   - Tighten in star pattern to ensure even pressure
   - Typical torque: 2-4 Nm (check manufacturer specifications)
   - Do not overtighten (can damage gasket or housing)

4. **Verify Seal:**
   - Inspect gasket compression (should be even all around)
   - Check no gaps visible between housing sections
   - Verify all screws are tight

**Quality Check:**
- Gasket properly seated
- Even compression around entire perimeter
- All screws tight
- No visible gaps

### 4.3 Connector Protection

**For External Connectors (network, power):**

**Procedure:**

1. **Protect Network Cable Connector:**
   - If using outdoor-rated connector, verify seal is intact
   - If using standard RJ45 connector:
     - Wrap connector with self-amalgamating tape
     - Start 50mm before connector, wrap tightly overlapping 50%
     - Extend wrap 50mm beyond connector
     - Apply additional layer of electrical tape over top
   - Alternative: Use waterproof junction box for connections

2. **Protect Power Connector:**
   - Similar wrapping procedure for exposed connectors
   - For DC power connections, use heat shrink tubing over crimped connections
   - Apply heat to shrink tubing completely
   - Wrap with self-amalgamating tape for additional protection

3. **Position Connectors:**
   - Orient connectors with opening facing downward if possible
   - Secure cables to prevent stress on connectors
   - Ensure connector protection does not interfere with camera adjustment

**Quality Check:**
- All exposed connectors sealed
- Wrapping is tight and complete
- Connectors oriented to shed water
- No stress on connections

### 4.4 Install Sunshade or Hood

**Purpose:** Reduces glare, protects lens, improves image quality

**Procedure:**

1. **Attach Sunshade:**
   - Mount sunshade to camera housing or lens
   - Ensure shade does not vignette (appear in) image
   - Adjust length to block sun at highest expected angle
   - Tighten mounting hardware

2. **Verify Effectiveness:**
   - Check test image for vignetting
   - Verify sunshade blocks direct sun during expected conditions
   - Adjust angle or length if needed

3. **Secure Sunshade:**
   - Tighten all mounting hardware
   - Verify sunshade will not move in wind
   - Check clearance for camera adjustment

## Step 5: Cable Routing

### 5.1 Route Cables Along Structure

**Goals:**
- Protect cables from weather and physical damage
- Provide neat, professional appearance
- Allow for cable movement (thermal expansion)
- Prevent water entry to housings

**Procedure:**

1. **Plan Cable Route:**
   - Route from camera to power/network connection point
   - Minimize exposure to direct sun and weather
   - Avoid sharp bends (minimum bend radius typically 4× cable diameter)
   - Keep away from moving parts or potential damage points

2. **Install Cable Support:**
   - Use UV-resistant cable ties every 300-500mm
   - Install cable clips or standoffs on mounting structure
   - Use conduit or cable tray for exposed runs if required
   - Ensure cables are supported but not over-tightened

3. **Create Service Loops:**
   - Leave extra cable length (1-2 meters) near camera
   - Coil excess neatly and secure
   - Allows for camera repositioning without running new cables
   - Label service loop location

**Quality Check:**
- Cables are neatly routed
- Adequate support along entire length
- No sharp bends or kinks
- Service loops provided
- Drip loops at all entry points

### 5.2 Ground Level Connection

**Procedure:**

1. **Connect to Power System:**
   - If using PoE: Connect to PoE switch or injector
   - If using separate DC power: Connect to power supply output
   - Verify correct polarity for DC connections
   - Install fuse in positive power line (typically 1-2A for IP cameras)

2. **Connect to Network:**
   - Connect Ethernet cable to network switch or recorder
   - Verify link LED illuminates
   - Test network connectivity

3. **Install Junction Box (if needed):**
   - Use weatherproof junction box for any ground-level connections
   - Mount securely above ground level
   - Seal all cable entries
   - Label connections inside box

**Quality Check:**
- All connections are secure and correct
- Polarity correct for DC power
- Network connectivity verified
- Junction boxes sealed

## Step 6: Initial Camera Configuration

### 6.1 Access Camera Interface

**Procedure:**

1. **Connect Laptop to Camera Network:**
   - Connect laptop to same network as camera
   - Configure laptop IP address in same subnet as camera
   - Open web browser

2. **Access Camera Web Interface:**
   - Enter camera IP address in browser
   - Login with default credentials (change these immediately)
   - Verify access to configuration pages

3. **Change Default Password:**
   - Navigate to user settings
   - Change admin password to strong password
   - Record new password in secure location
   - Consider creating additional user accounts with limited privileges

### 6.2 Configure Network Settings

**Procedure:**

1. **Set Static IP Address (recommended):**
   - Navigate to network settings
   - Assign static IP address from site network plan
   - Set subnet mask and gateway
   - Configure DNS servers if required
   - Save and reboot camera

2. **Verify Network Configuration:**
   - Ping camera from laptop
   - Access camera interface at new IP address
   - Verify network connectivity is stable

3. **Configure Network Security:**
   - Enable HTTPS if available
   - Configure firewall rules if camera has built-in firewall
   - Disable unused network services
   - Set allowed IP addresses if camera supports this

### 6.3 Configure Image Settings

**Basic Settings (detailed configuration in Section 8.4):**

1. **Set Resolution:**
   - Select resolution matching site plan requirements
   - Higher resolution for detailed measurements
   - Lower resolution reduces bandwidth and storage
   - Typical: 1920×1080 (Full HD) or higher

2. **Set Frame Rate:**
   - For river monitoring: 1-5 fps typically sufficient
   - Higher frame rate increases bandwidth and storage
   - Set to match recording requirements

3. **Configure Compression:**
   - Select codec (H.264 or H.265 recommended)
   - Set quality/bitrate
   - Balance image quality vs. bandwidth/storage

4. **Adjust Exposure:**
   - Set to automatic for initial setup
   - May require manual adjustment for specific conditions
   - Fine-tuning in Section 8.4

**Save Configuration:**
- Save all settings
- Export configuration file as backup
- Store configuration file with installation documentation

### 6.4 Test Camera Operation

**Procedure:**

1. **Verify Live View:**
   - Access live video stream
   - Verify image is clear and stable
   - Check frame rate matches configuration
   - Verify no errors or warnings

2. **Capture Test Images:**
   - Save several test images
   - Verify images are stored correctly
   - Check image file size and quality
   - Confirm timestamp is correct (set camera time/date if needed)

3. **Verify Continuous Operation:**
   - Let camera run for 30-60 minutes
   - Check for any interruptions or errors
   - Verify network connection remains stable
   - Check power consumption is within expected range

**Quality Check:**
- Camera accessible via network
- Live stream is clear and stable
- Images are being captured correctly
- No errors or warnings
- Power consumption normal

## Troubleshooting Guide

### Problem: Camera Not Powering On

**Solutions:**
1. Check power connection at camera
2. Verify power supply output voltage
3. Check fuses in power line
4. Test cable for continuity
5. Verify PoE compatibility (voltage and wattage)

### Problem: No Network Connection

**Solutions:**
1. Verify Ethernet cable is connected firmly
2. Check link LED on camera and switch
3. Test cable with cable tester
4. Verify camera IP address configuration
5. Check network switch is powered and functioning
6. Verify PoE power delivery if using PoE

### Problem: Blurry or Out of Focus Image

**Solutions:**
1. Clean camera lens
2. Adjust focus (manually or auto-focus)
3. Check for condensation inside housing (add desiccant)
4. Verify lens is not damaged
5. Check that protective film removed from lens

### Problem: Poor Image Quality

**Solutions:**
1. Adjust exposure settings
2. Check lighting conditions
3. Verify lens is clean
4. Adjust compression settings (reduce compression)
5. Check for sunlight glare (adjust sunshade)
6. Verify adequate bandwidth for selected resolution

### Problem: Water Ingress in Housing

**Solutions:**
1. Check all housing seals and gaskets
2. Verify cable glands are tight
3. Inspect for cracks or damage to housing
4. Ensure drip loops are formed correctly
5. Check sealant around cable entries
6. Dry housing thoroughly and replace desiccant

### Problem: Camera Aim Shifted

**Solutions:**
1. Check mounting hardware is tight
2. Verify bracket adjustment bolts are locked
3. Apply thread-locking compound to adjustment bolts
4. Re-aim camera and lock more securely
5. Consider additional vibration damping if structure vibrates

## Maintenance Schedule

**Daily (First Week):**
- Check camera image quality
- Verify network connectivity
- Check for any physical damage

**Weekly (First Month):**
- Verify aim has not shifted
- Check all mounting hardware is tight
- Inspect weatherproofing
- Clean lens if needed

**Monthly:**
- Clean camera lens and housing
- Check and tighten all connections
- Verify image quality remains good
- Inspect cables for damage
- Check desiccant condition

**Quarterly:**
- Inspect all weatherproofing
- Verify mounting hardware tight
- Check housing seals
- Update firmware if available
- Test all camera functions

**Annually:**
- Complete system inspection
- Replace desiccant
- Inspect and replace damaged cables
- Check bracket and mounting structure for corrosion
- Review and update configuration

## Summary

Camera installation requires careful attention to mounting, aiming, weatherproofing, and initial configuration. Key success factors include:

1. **Secure Mounting:** Camera must remain stable in all weather conditions
2. **Accurate Aiming:** Proper aim ensures staff gauge is visible and centered
3. **Complete Weatherproofing:** All cable entries and connections must be sealed
4. **Quality Connections:** Network and power connections must be reliable
5. **Initial Configuration:** Basic camera settings configured for site conditions

A properly installed camera will provide clear, stable images for accurate river monitoring and water level measurement.
